Capisaan Cave System

The Capisaan Cave System in the municipality of Kasibu is considered one of the longest and most impressive cave systems in the Philippines. The cave stretches for kilometers underground, featuring breathtaking geological formations including stalactites, stalagmites, flowstones, and underground rivers.

The cave is home to thousands of bats and a variety of cave-dwelling species. Guided spelunking tours are available and are the only way to explore this magnificent natural wonder safely.

Activities at Capisaan Cave

🕳️
Spelunking

Explore the cave's chambers and tunnels with a certified local guide. Multiple cave routes cater to different experience levels, from easy walks to challenging crawls.

📸
Photography

Capture the stunning geological formations lit by natural and artificial light. Bring a good camera and extra batteries — the formations are extraordinary.

🦇
Bat Watching

Witness thousands of bats emerge from the cave entrance at dusk in a spectacular natural display. The bat colonies are an important part of the cave ecosystem.

💡 Before You Go

  • Wear sturdy closed-toe shoes with good grip — cave floors can be slippery
  • Guided tours are mandatory; solo entry is not permitted
  • Bring a headlamp or flashlight as backup even if guides carry lights
  • Avoid loose or flowing clothing that can catch on formations
  • Do not touch or break cave formations — they take thousands of years to form
