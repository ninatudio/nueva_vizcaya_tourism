Festivals of Nueva Vizcaya

From grand provincial festivals to intimate indigenous celebrations, Nueva Vizcaya's cultural calendar is rich and diverse year-round.

April

Ammungan Festival

📍 Location: Bayombong (Provincial Capitol Grounds)

🗓️ Month: April (week-long celebration)

The Ammungan Festival is the grandest cultural celebration in Nueva Vizcaya, held every April to coincide with the province's founding anniversary. The word "ammungan" means "gathering" or "meeting place" in the indigenous Isinai language — a fitting name for a festival that unites all of the province's municipalities and ethnic groups in one vibrant celebration.

Highlights include spectacular street dancing competitions where performers in traditional indigenous costumes interpret the history and culture of each municipality, trade fairs showcasing local products (especially citrus and highland crafts), agri-fair exhibits, sports competitions, and cultural shows featuring the Bugkalot, Gaddang, Isinai, Kalanguya, and Ifugao communities.

🎯 Festival Highlights

  • Grand street dancing competition with indigenous-themed performances
  • Agri-trade fair and citrus industry showcase
  • Cultural presentations by indigenous groups
  • Sports events and tournaments
  • Beauty pageant (Miss Ammungan)
  • Fireworks display and evening concerts
January

Pattong Festival

📍 Location: Aritao, Nueva Vizcaya

🗓️ Month: January (New Year Celebration)

The Pattong Festival is the traditional New Year celebration of the Isinai indigenous people of Aritao. It is one of the oldest indigenous cultural celebrations still observed in Nueva Vizcaya, marked by communal rituals, traditional dances, indigenous music, and the sharing of locally prepared food.

The festival serves as a time of communal bonding, thanksgiving for the past year's harvest, and prayer for prosperity in the coming year. Elders perform rituals while younger generations participate in traditional dances and songs, ensuring the continuity of Isinai cultural practices.

🎯 Festival Highlights

  • Traditional Isinai ritual ceremonies led by elders
  • Indigenous music with bamboo instruments and gongs
  • Communal thanksgiving feast with traditional food
  • Blessing of seeds and farming tools for the coming season
  • Traditional weaving and crafts demonstrations
May

Bambang Town Fiesta

📍 Location: Bambang, Nueva Vizcaya

🗓️ Month: May 3 (Feast of the Holy Cross)

Bambang is the most densely populated municipality in Nueva Vizcaya and its annual fiesta in honor of the Holy Cross is one of the liveliest town celebrations in the province. The fiesta blends Catholic devotion with indigenous cultural expressions unique to the Gaddang communities of Bambang.

Festivities include a solemn procession, High Mass, street dancing in brightly decorated costumes, a trade fair, and community games. Local food stalls line the streets, offering traditional Gaddang delicacies and dishes that visitors can sample throughout the celebration.

🎯 Festival Highlights

  • Solemn procession and High Mass at the parish church
  • Street dancing competitions with Gaddang-themed costumes
  • Trade fair with local products and crafts
  • Traditional Gaddang food and delicacies on display
  • Community sports events and parlor games
November

Panagyaman (Thanksgiving) Festival

📍 Location: Kayapa, Nueva Vizcaya

🗓️ Month: November (Post-Harvest Season)

The Panagyaman Festival is the post-harvest thanksgiving celebration of the Kalanguya indigenous people of Kayapa, one of the highest municipalities in Nueva Vizcaya. The word "panagyaman" means "thanksgiving" in the Kalanguya language, and the festival is a deeply spiritual occasion marking the end of the rice harvest season.

Elders perform traditional rituals of gratitude to the spirits of nature, while the community shares communal meals of traditional food prepared using the newly harvested rice. Traditional music played on gangsa (gongs), flutes, and other indigenous instruments fills the highland air.

🎯 Festival Highlights

  • Traditional Kalanguya harvest thanksgiving rituals
  • Gangsa (gong) music and traditional highland dances
  • Communal feast featuring traditional highland dishes
  • Demonstrations of traditional Kalanguya weaving
  • Elder-led storytelling and oral history sharing
