Nueva Vizcaya: Citrus Capital of the Philippines

Nueva Vizcaya has earned the prestigious title of Citrus Capital of the Philippines, and it's a distinction the province wears with great pride. The cool highland climate of the province's interior municipalities — particularly Kayapa, Ambaguio, and Santa Fe — creates ideal growing conditions for citrus fruits that are sweeter, juicier, and more aromatic than those grown in lowland areas.

Citrus farming has been a cornerstone of the provincial economy for decades. Today, thousands of farming families depend on citrus orchards for their livelihood, and the industry contributes significantly to the province's gross domestic product. Nueva Vizcaya citrus products are exported to Metro Manila, neighboring provinces, and even processed into juices, candies, and marmalades sold throughout the country.

Citrus Varieties of Nueva Vizcaya

🍊
Ponkan (Mandarin Orange)

The most famous citrus product of Nueva Vizcaya. Ponkan is a loose-skinned mandarin orange that is exceptionally sweet and seedless, with a rich, fragrant aroma. Peak season runs from November to January. Kayapa ponkan is considered the finest variety in the Philippines.

🟠
Dalandan (Philippine Orange)

Dalandan is the Philippine native orange, with a characteristically green or orange-green skin and a wonderfully tangy-sweet juice. Often processed into fresh juice or sold whole. Dalandan from the Nueva Vizcaya highlands is prized for its balanced sweetness and high juice content. Peak season: October to January.

🍋
Suha (Pomelo)

Suha is a large, pale-fleshed citrus fruit related to the grapefruit. Nueva Vizcaya's highland suha is known for its particularly thick pith, crisp texture, and mild, slightly sweet flavor. It is enjoyed as a snack, used in salads, or pressed for juice. Peak season: December to February.

💛
Calamansi (Philippine Lime)

While grown throughout the Philippines, the highland calamansi of Nueva Vizcaya is notably more flavorful and aromatic. Used ubiquitously in Filipino cooking as a souring agent, beverage ingredient, and dipping sauce enhancer. Available year-round from farm plots in Bambang and Bayombong municipalities.

🟡
Lemon

Introduced varieties of lemon are now commercially grown in select highland areas of Nueva Vizcaya. The cool temperatures produce lemons with a high acid content and intense fragrance. Used in culinary applications and increasingly processed into lemon-flavored preserves and beverages by local enterprises.

🌿
Citrus Products & Derivatives

Beyond fresh fruits, Nueva Vizcaya's entrepreneurial farmers produce a range of citrus-derived products including ponkan jam, dalandan marmalade, citrus candies, dried citrus peel, flavored vinegar, and citrus-infused cooking sauces — all available at markets and pasalubong shops throughout the province.

Visiting Citrus Farms

📍 Key Farm Areas

  • Kayapa: Highest concentration of ponkan orchards; most famous for premium quality fruit
  • Ambaguio: Accessible highland farm area with a mix of ponkan, dalandan, and suha
  • Santa Fe: Combined citrus and vegetable farms; home to the Paitan Flower Farm
  • Bambang: Lower-elevation calamansi and dalandan production

🌾 Agri-Tourism Tips

  • Visit November to February for the fullest selection of ripe citrus
  • Coordinate with the Provincial Agriculture Office or local tourism office for organized farm tours
  • Bring cash — most farm stands and small vendors do not accept cards
  • Buy directly from farmers to support local families and get the freshest produce
  • Check if the farm offers pick-your-own fruit experiences — some farms allow visitors to harvest directly
